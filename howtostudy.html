How to Study

When preparing for the exam, start with the fundamentals. Aim to get your practice exam scores up to 80-85% on 100-question practice exams in every category, as this is a good indicator of success on the actual exam.

First, learn the vocabulary well because it's essential for understanding the exam. Then, take practice exams to test and sharpen your knowledge. Spend a couple of weeks on each of these steps to improve your scores.

While taking practice exams, focus on understanding what each question is testing rather than just memorizing the questions. Connect each question to a keyword, vocabulary term, or concept. If you don't understand why you got a question wrong, pause your practice exam and research the topic. This way, you'll be better prepared to understand the real exam, regardless of tricky wording or confusing formats.
